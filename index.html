About

Black Cats Appreciation Society is focused on connecting adopters with local black cats through a simple directory and celebrates their unique personalities in a vibrant user-driven photo gallery.

By spotlighting real companion cats and simplifying the search and adoption journey, we aim to change hearts and minds - one photo, one adoption, one myth at a time

Black Cat Mythology, Debunked

Black Cat Myths Busted!

Myth Image Fact
Black cats are bad luck. Pandora was rescued, fostered, and given a second chance bringing nothing but joy and affectionate head tilts. She was a magical box of love, hence the name. Superstition? Debunked.
Black cats don't get adopted. Dresden was adopted and adored like thousands of other black cats when given the chance. Awareness and love make the difference.
Seeing a black cat cross your path is a bad omen. In many cultures, black cats are symbols of luck, love, or protection not omens. The real bad luck is the superstition itself.
